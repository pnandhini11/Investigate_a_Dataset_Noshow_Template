Introduction¶

In this dataset, we'll be analyzing a little over 100k medical appointments in Brazil focusing on whether or not the patients showed up for their appointment.

There will be several factors to consider such as chronic diseases or conditions (alcoholism, diabetes, hypertension, and handicap), demographics (such as age and gender), and others. The goal is to determine whether or not these factors have a direct correlation with patients showing up or not showing up to their appointment.

Note:

  • "ScheduledDay": is the day the patient set up their appointment
  • "Neighbourhood": is the location of the hospital
  • "Scholarship": indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in a Brasilian Welfare program called Bolsa Familia

Now let's dive deep into the dataset and find out the answers to these questions.

Conclusions¶

To sum up all of this:

Neighbourhood: Seems to be strongly correlated to patients showing up for their appointment.
Age: Certain age groups seemed to show up more than others such as those below 10 years old and those between ages 35 and 70.
SMS_received: While it may not be directly correlated, it was surprising to learn that more people showed up without receiving an SMS reminder.

Limitations:
We couldn't find a direct correlation between shows/no shows and the other factors like gender, diabetes, hypertension, handicap, welfare, and alcoholism.
